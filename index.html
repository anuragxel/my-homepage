Hello There!

I'm Anurag Ghosh, a Junior Year Undergrad living in Hyderabad.

I'm pursuing my B.Tech and MS by Research in Computer Science and Engineering at Centre for Visual Information Technology, International Institute of Information Technology - Hyderabad (started 2013).

I'm advised by Professor CV Jawahar and I work primarly on problems related to Computer Vision and Machine Learning.

I love to sprint, draw, quiz and write. I also help out with Ping!, the college magazine, you may want to have a look at our articles. I also contribute to the Open Source Initiative through my contribution to Boost C++ Libraries

I was coordinating Felicity LitCafe in Felicity'16. I'm currently coordinating student placement activities at IIIT Hyderabad along with handling fresher's induction program since last year.

I have TA-ed Discrete Mathematics (IMA101, a fresher's compulsary course) in Monsoon 2015, it was the best job in the world! Looking forward to another teaching stint this Monsoon semester. :)

My primary research interests lie in Computer Vision and Machine Learning. I'm also interested in Image Processing and Artificial Intelligence, I love building cool stuff and working on exciting problems.

I have been currently working on Dynamic Narrative Generation with Yash guided by Dr. Jawahar and Mohak. The project aims to provide a user (equiped with a mobile device) with stories relevant to a heritage site. For instance, if you are near Charminar, you will be listening to the story of eradication of plague and subsquent construction of this landmark. (Gruesome example, I know, I know)

I have been working on building Recommendation Systems with Arpit and Vatika. The hybrid system involves aspects from Collaborative Filtering, Social Based and Content Based Filters to give diverse recommendations.

Earlier, Vatika, Abhijeet and I were dabbling with Spectral Learning to Grade Essays Automatically. Hopeful of getting back to this sooner enough and making this more concrete.

My first foray into Computer Vision was when I helped out in developing a Scene Text Translation Application. It worked on a KNN + Bag of Words scheme to retrieve the relavent sign board from our repository and thus the translation. We showed it off in Kumbathon, you can watch the demo here.

Apart from my research projects, I have been pretty intensely involved in a Boost C++ Libraries funded project (through Google Summer of Code 2015 and subsequently in 2016, by Boost Steering Committee), where I'm building a Document Automation Library mentored by Antony Polukhin. The Library automates many of the Excel/Libreoffice tasks, like making, filling and exporting spreadsheets in C++, is cross platform and well tested.

I am fond of another project, done with Parth, a teaching tool for students of Digital Logic and Processors. It is a block level and cycle level simulator of a processor along with an intruction set assembler for the associated assembly language. The tool is currently a command line one and we are building a GUI for easier access . We previously took a neat lecture on Microprogram Sequencers for UG2k14, which was fun! We are currently writing a handout explaining how simple processors work, message me if you have any good ideas! :)

I built a system with Romil for very high dimensional fuzzy searching based on a very cool research paper which explains a two level scheme, clustering and then data oriented hashing. We tested it on the GIST dataset to great effect.

I have also built a couple of interpreters on my own, some for Esoteric Languages like Brainfuck and SNUSP, others for a lisp-like language I call vhisp based on this cool tutorial. I also have a mini sql engine and a pretty fast external merge sort algorithm lying around just to exercise my C++ skills.

I have been invovled in setting up the Attendance Infrastructure of IIIT-H which I have developed with Yash. A nice and pretty safe android BookMyShow-esque application which communicates with a server to help a guy take attendance as all book keeping is now managed automatically. I have been managing the Fresher's Registration Portal since the last two years.

I'm one of the Atom cluster sysadmins here, if you need something, you may ask me (or not).

I was also a Student Representative of UG2k13 in the Student's Parliament. I worked on changing the college laptop policy (which was modified for the upcoming batch), getting a batch hoodie made and handled cases of hostel violation among other things.

I have been previously involved in the Threads team, helping out with the platform and sometimes the questions. More recently, I have shifted my priorities towards LitCafe after hosting the LitQuiz last year.
